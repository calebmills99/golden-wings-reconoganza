## Introduction

The allure of the Silicon Beach Film Festival first captured my attention as I browsed through the myriad of festivals listed on Film Freeway. As an elder millennial filmmaker, the intersection of documentary storytelling and technology has always intrigued me. Submitting my 22-minute documentary seemed like a long shot, but it turned out to be an opportunity that would propel my personal project into the limelight of one of the most iconic venues in Hollywood—the Chinese Theater.

![](https://indiedocjourney.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/08/illustrative_albedo_the_historic_chinese_theater_in_hollywood_0-2.jpg?w=1024)

## The Origins: A Student Project

The documentary that would eventually capture the attention of the Silicon Beach Film Festival had humble beginnings. It started as a simple student project in an introductory documentary filmmaking class, guided by the accomplished documentarian Janice Engle. My initial footage was minimal—a 20-minute interview with my mother and a handful of B-roll shots from a visit to the CR Smith Museum. This raw, unpolished material was far from the makings of a full-length film. However, with a passion for storytelling and a deep-seated need to explore what it means to carry on a family legacy as someone without children, I set out to transform this modest beginning into a 22-minute documentary that would resonate with audiences.

![](https://indiedocjourney.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/08/robyn-stewart-interview.png?w=1024)

## Exploring Legacy and Identity

One of the central themes of this documentary is the question of how a person without children can carry on their family legacy. This theme is deeply personal to me, not just as a filmmaker, but as someone who has always pondered what it means to leave a mark on the world when the traditional paths of legacy—through children—are not an option.

![](https://indiedocjourney.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/08/grad.png?w=950)

**A Personal Journey**

In many ways, this documentary became a reflection of my own journey. As a 43-year-old gay man, I've often felt like an "old maid," a term that carries with it a mixture of humor and melancholy. Without children of my own, I’ve wondered how I can contribute to my family’s history and preserve the stories that have shaped me. This film became a medium to explore these questions, using my family's deep connection to American Airlines as a narrative thread that ties past, present, and future.

**Family Stories and Legacy**

My mother, who was one of the first flight attendants trained on the Boeing 747, and my grandfather, who created the pilot training program at American Airlines, are central figures in this documentary. Their stories, combined with over 88 years of family service to American Airlines, represent a legacy that I’ve sought to honor and preserve. Through this film, I explore not just their contributions, but also what it means to inherit and carry forward a legacy when the baton is passed in ways other than through biological children.

![](https://indiedocjourney.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/08/untitled-1cr-smith.png?w=1024)

## Leveraging Technology for Storytelling

Creating a compelling 22-minute documentary from such sparse footage required ingenuity and a deep dive into the available technological resources. Each tool I employed played a crucial role in expanding the narrative and bringing the story to life.

**Archival Footage Research**

The visual narrative needed depth, and I found that depth in archival footage. The Internet Archive became an invaluable resource, offering a treasure trove of vintage airline footage that perfectly complemented my story. I also scoured platforms like Periscope, which surprisingly held content that resonated with the themes of my documentary. Perhaps the most exciting finds were the copyright-free Boeing industrial films that showcased early aircraft, providing a historical context that enriched the narrative.

![](https://indiedocjourney.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/08/1971-american-airlines-747-postcard.jpg?w=1024)

**Stock Resources**

Next, I turned to Envato Elements for stock resources that would add visual interest and cohesiveness to the film. This platform offered an array of stock footage, images, and transitions that seamlessly integrated with my existing footage. Moreover, it inspired new storyboard sequences that enhanced the overall storytelling, making the documentary more engaging and visually dynamic.

https://vimeo.com/995184923

**Adobe Creative Suite**

The Adobe Creative Suite became my technological Swiss Army knife. After Effects allowed me to create special effects that added a layer of sophistication to the documentary. InDesign was indispensable for designing the marketing materials needed to promote the film, while Audition enabled me to record and edit the voiceover narration, ensuring that the audio quality matched the visual standard. Photoshop played a crucial role in restoring old family photos, which were then integrated into the documentary to add a personal touch.

![](https://indiedocjourney.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/08/default_a_highly_detailed_colorful_screenshot_of_a_professiona_1-1.jpg?w=1024)

**Social Media Engagement**

Recognizing the importance of building an audience, I harnessed the power of social media to test and promote my content. I created short segments specifically for TikTok, using the hashtag #TikTokDoc to reach a broader audience. These mini-stories, ranging from one to four minutes, totaled about 20 minutes of content and served as both promotional material and a way to gauge audience interest and engagement.

## Overcoming Challenges

Creating a documentary is never without its challenges, and this project was no exception. The most significant hurdle was achieving narrative cohesion, especially with the limited footage I had at the start.

**Narrative Cohesion**

To bridge the gaps in the story, I made two crucial decisions. First, I added voiceover narration to tie the scenes together, ensuring that the narrative flowed smoothly from one segment to the next. Second, I conducted extensive research on Boeing and the history of aviation, which provided the necessary historical context to support and enrich the personal story I was telling. (The clip below shows my very simple VO recording setup.)

**Balancing Personal and Historical Narratives**

One of the challenges in making this documentary was balancing the personal and historical narratives. While the film is deeply rooted in my family’s history with American Airlines, it also needed to speak to broader themes of legacy and identity. This balance was crucial in making the documentary relatable to a wider audience, beyond just those interested in aviation history.

## The Road to Silicon Beach

The journey to the Silicon Beach Film Festival was both thrilling and nerve-wracking. Submitting my documentary was a leap of faith, especially considering that I sent in a rough cut, prioritizing meeting the submission deadline over perfecting the film.

**Submission Process**

About a month ago, I submitted the documentary to the Silicon Beach Film Festival. At that point, it was still very much a work in progress. The rough cut I submitted was, by my own admission, far from perfect, but I knew I had to take the chance. After all, meeting the deadline was crucial, and I always refine the film if it were accepted.

**Unexpected Acceptance**

I’ll never forget the moment I received the acceptance call. It was a Tuesday, and I was going about my day, not expecting anything out of the ordinary. When the festival organizer informed me that my 22-minute documentary had been selected as an official entry, I was stunned. Shock and disbelief washed over me as I processed the news—it felt surreal that my project had made it this far.

![](https://indiedocjourney.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/08/default_an_insert_shot_of_a_samsung_gaklaxy_s23_a_film_docum_2.jpg?w=1024)

**Honest Communication**

Despite my excitement, I couldn't rid myself of the deep concern I harbored regarding the state of the film I had submitted. As I spoke during the call, I openly voiced my apprehensions to the organizer, characterizing the submitted version as "rough as sandpaper." I received reassurance that submitting a finalized cut at a later time would suffice, affording me the opportunity to refine the documentary to a standard with which I could truly be pleased.

**Reflections on the Selection**

The acceptance of my 22-minute documentary made me realize the true power of storytelling. Even with its technical imperfections—audio inconsistencies, color correction needs, and other refinements pending—the story resonated. It was a moment of clarity, where I understood that while technical expertise is important, the emotional and narrative strength of a film can often carry it through.

**A Dream Come True**

Knowing that my 22-minute documentary will be screened at the Chinese Theater in Hollywood is a dream come true. It’s an opportunity that many filmmakers aspire to but few achieve, and I’m acutely aware of the responsibility that comes with it. The grandeur of the Chinese Theater, with its rich history and association with the golden age of Hollywood, adds a layer of prestige that makes this moment all the more special.

![](https://indiedocjourney.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/08/chinese.png?w=640)

## Conclusion

Reflecting on this journey from a student project to a festival-selected 22-minute documentary, I’m filled with gratitude and a sense of accomplishment. At 43, I’ve embraced the idea that legacy isn’t just about children—it’s about the stories we tell and the impact we leave on those who hear them. This project has been both a challenge and a revelation, showing me that while I may not have children to carry on my family name, I can still contribute to the legacy of those who came before me.

This opportunity to share my work with a broader audience at the Silicon Beach Film Festival is both humbling and exhilarating. I look forward to the screening at the Chinese Theater and am excited about the possibilities that lie ahead.

Thank you for taking the time to read about this journey. Your support and interest mean the world to me. Here’s to the power of storytelling and the endless possibilities that lie within every creative endeavor.
